Nongprue Culture

Although primarily known as an international tourist destination, with its local population catering to tourism in it's city center, Nongprue retains some key Thai cultural aspects throughout. Inside the tourism zone, traditional street foods are available for locals and foreigners alike. In the center of the city, various renditions of popular songs of the West can be heard as well as pop Thai music. Outside of the center of town, life begins to normalize with traditional foods and music.

Traditional Sounds

Thai Morlum

Thai Morlum is a traditional form of music common in Laos and the Northeastern region of Thailand. Many people come to work in Nongprue and make the areas not bustling with tourists feel more like home.

Street food

Banana Pancakes

Street foods are very popular and for good reason. They are drastically less expensive than restaurant foods and quite tasty and of fresh quality with a regular flow of customers.
